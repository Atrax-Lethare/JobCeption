## Purpose
Define what is in and out of scope for each phase of JobCeption, plus the constraints under which the team operates.

## Phase 1 — MVP (Months 0–4)
### In Scope
- Candidate and recruiter accounts; verified identity for recruiters.
- Dynamic Profile (basic): header, stats, bounties tab, auto-CV.
- One bounty type at launch (suggest: code or design).
- Recruiter Layer-1 binding form, Layer-2 template choice, basic Layer-3 constraints.
- Sandbox runtime for one bounty type.
- Progress Indicator with Pledge Pop-ups.
- Aura Points (basic formula).
- Web (responsive) only.

### Out of Scope
- Connections / Posts / Feed.
- Creative Aura Points.
- Native mobile apps.
- Team bounties.
- Multi-language UI.
- Public API.

## Phase 2 — Trust Layer (Months 4–8)
- Reviews, dispute resolution, full reputation system.
- Bounty templates library expansion.
- Freelance escrow.
- Two more bounty types in the sandbox.

## Phase 3 — Social Layer (Months 8–12)
- Connections feed, badges, Creative XP, spam folder.

## Phase 4 — Scale (Year 2)
- Mobile apps, smart matching, team bounties, localization, public API.

## Constraints
- **Budget:** TBD by founder.
- **Team size at MVP:** small (assume 3–6 engineers).
- **Geographic launch:** India-first (DPDP-compliant by default).
- **Legal:** pledges must be enforceable in launch jurisdiction.
- **Hosting:** cloud (AWS or GCP — see `07-infrastructure-spec.md`).

## Assumptions
- Candidates will accept that bounties take real time in exchange for fair evaluation.
- Recruiters will pay for access to high-signal candidates.
- Companies will accept reputational accountability in exchange for hire quality.

## Open Questions
- Single vertical at launch or horizontal?
- Self-serve or sales-led for recruiter onboarding?
